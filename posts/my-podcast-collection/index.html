My Podcast Journey#

In this era of information explosion, efficiently acquiring knowledge and continuous learning has become an important topic. This article shares my experience of using podcasts for English learning and expanding my technical horizons, hoping to provide some reference for friends who want to try listening to podcasts.

Background: Loads of Free Time Brought by the Pandemic#

The 2020 pandemic changed the way many people work, with remote and hybrid work models becoming mainstream. This change brought more free time at home and during commutes. Facing the uncertainty of China’s socio-economic prospects, I decided to use this time to improve my competitiveness, especially in English language skills.

Initially, I chose popular programs like TED and BBC Global News as learning resources. However, I soon discovered some limitations in this content:

  1. Topics are relatively single
  2. Language difficulty is lower
  3. Hard to satisfy the need for in-depth understanding of English language culture

To break through these limitations, I started to use search engines and the recommendation systems of podcast apps like Pocket Casts to explore the broader world of podcasts. This decision opened up my podcast learning journey full of surprises.

My Podcast List#

After a period of exploration and screening, my podcast subscription list gradually formed the following four major categories:

  1. Music
  2. Finance
  3. Technology
  4. English Learning

Music#

My taste in music is quite omnivorous, covering:

  • Western Pop and Rock
  • Mandarin Pop Songs
  • Cantonese Oldies
  • Japanese Anime Songs

Finance#

In the finance field, I mainly focus on the following types of podcasts:

  1. Historical stories, analysis, and news of companies and financial markets

    • The Economist
    • Planet Money
    • Freakonomics Radio
    • Acquired

  2. Investment Strategies and Personal Finance

    • Motley Fool Money
    • We Study Billionaires - The Investor’s Podcast Network
    • The Money To The Masses Podcast
    • MarketPlace

These podcasts help me understand the current macroeconomic situation, corporate history, current investment strategies, etc.

Technology: Keeping Up with the Frontier#

As a programmer, I pay special attention to podcasts related to programming languages, software development, and cybersecurity:

  1. Programming Language Specials

    • Python: Talk Python To Me, Python People
    • Go: Go Time, Go Nightly Chat
    • JavaScript: JS Party

  2. Site Reliability Engineering (SRE)

    • Ship It!

  3. Cybersecurity

    • Security Weekly Podcast Network
    • Darknet Diaries

  4. Tech Media

    • The Verge
    • Wired
    • a16z Podcast
    • Marketplace Tech

  5. Chinese Tech Podcasts

    • Beyond Code
    • Python Hunter
    • Software Talk
    • Avocado Toast

These podcasts not only help me review best practices in programming languages but also let me understand the latest technology trends and corporate technical practices.

English Learning#

To systematically improve my English skills, I chose the following types of podcasts:

  1. IELTS Preparation

    • IELTS Podcast
    • IELTS Speaking for Success
    • IELTS Energy English 7+

  2. Daily and Business English

    • Learning English Conversations
    • Learning English Grammar
    • Learning English Vocabulary
    • Culips Everyday English Podcast
    • Business English Pod

Gains: English Proficiency Improvement and Tech Horizon Expansion#

Through continuous podcast learning, I have made significant progress:

  1. English vocabulary reached about 15,000 words
  2. Significant improvement in English oral communication skills
  3. Successfully passed English technical interviews for some foreign companies

In terms of technology, my gains are equally rich:

  1. Reviewed and deepened understanding of programming languages, DB, SRE, cybersecurity, etc.
  2. Understood frontier technology trends: LLM, Cloud Native, etc.

Suggestions: Customize Your Own Podcast Journey#

If you also want to improve yourself through podcasts, here are some of my suggestions:

  1. Start with Interest: Choose topics you are interested in, so it’s easier to persist.
  2. Step by Step: You can start with podcasts in your native language and gradually transition to English podcasts.
  3. Explore More: Try different types of podcasts to broaden your knowledge.
